The problem with most voice AI in India

English models don't learn Hindi. They approximate it.

01

Most speech AI was trained on English — and bolted Hindi on top.

It was fine-tuned on a handful of Indian-language hours to check the "multilingual" box. The result: models that stumble on Indian accents, collapse on telephony audio, and produce Hindi that sounds like it was translated from a phrasebook.

02

Stitching vendors together multiplies your errors.

Run one provider's STT into another's TTS and every error upstream compounds downstream. The bot pauses in the wrong places. Proper nouns get mangled. Customers hang up.

03

Your customers don't speak textbook Hindi.

They speak the Hindi of Patna, Raipur, Kanpur, Hyderabad — mixed with English, on 8 kHz phone lines, through background noise. Your voicebot needs a speech engine built for exactly that.

Your questions, answered

Everything you'd ask on a first call.

What languages does Tarang support?
Tarang STT covers 9 Indian languages — Hindi, English (Indian), Tamil, Telugu, Marathi, Bengali, Gujarati, Kannada, Punjabi — plus 4 Hindi dialects (Bhojpuri, Chhattisgarhi, Magahi, Maithili). Tarang TTS covers all 22 Scheduled Indian languages plus regional dialects including Haryanvi. Automatic language detection on a single stream is supported.
Does Tarang handle code-switching between Hindi and English?
Yes. Tarang is trained on real Indian speech, which means natural Hindi-English code-switching, loan words, and mid-sentence language swaps are handled without breaking transcription.
Can I use Tarang for real-time voice agents?
Yes. Streaming STT runs at ~150 ms latency over WebSocket — fast enough for live conversational agents. Streaming TTS delivers 200–250 ms time-to-first-byte. Pipecat and LiveKit integrations are available.
Does Tarang work on telephony audio?
Yes, natively. Tarang supports 8 kHz μ-law audio out of the box — no up-sampling, no intermediate conversion. The model was trained and evaluated on real PSTN and SIP call data, not just studio recordings.
What deployment options are available?
Ori-hosted on AWS ap-south-1 (Mumbai) by default. BYOC available on your AWS or GCP account for production commitments. Your audio never leaves your cloud on BYOC, and stays stateless on the hosted option.
What about compliance and data residency?
All data stays in India. Tarang's architecture is aligned with DPDP, RBI, and IRDAI requirements. TLS 1.2+ in transit, AES-256 at rest, RBAC least-privilege, tenant-isolated storage, customer-managed keys on BYOC.
Can I clone a voice for my brand?
Yes. Voice cloning requires just 5 seconds of reference audio. Once cloned, the voice speaks across all 22 Indian languages — so your brand voice in Hindi can also speak in Tamil, Bengali, or Marathi without re-recording.
